CODEV2E Terms and Conditions

1. All projects created under CODEV2E must prioritize storytelling quality over visual spectacle.

2. Every team member is required to maintain production discipline and respect studio workflow.

3. Scripts, storyboards, and visual concepts must receive approval before animation production begins.

4. Unauthorized redesigns of characters, environments, or visual identity are prohibited.

5. All assets created for studio projects remain studio property unless officially agreed otherwise.

6. Plagiarism, tracing, or unauthorized copying of external work is strictly prohibited.

7. Team members must maintain file organization and follow official naming systems.

8. Missed deadlines without communication may result in removal from production responsibilities.

9. Personal ego must never interfere with project quality or team collaboration.

10. Harassment, disrespectful behavior, or public humiliation inside the team environment is prohibited.

11. Story changes after production approval require director authorization.

12. Animation shortcuts that reduce visual quality are not permitted.

13. Artificial emotional manipulation without narrative purpose is discouraged.

14. Dialogue must remain natural and character-consistent throughout production.

15. Every project must maintain visual consistency from beginning to completion.

16. Backgrounds, sound, and cinematography are considered essential storytelling elements.

17. Audio quality standards must be maintained in every production stage.

18. Team members are expected to continue improving artistic and technical skills.

19. Leaking unreleased studio content without permission is prohibited.

20. Use of AI-generated assets without official approval is prohibited.

21. Projects must avoid unnecessary filler content that weakens pacing or storytelling.

22. Character behavior and emotional progression must remain internally consistent.

23. All action choreography must remain visually readable and understandable.

24. Last-minute production changes that risk project stability should be avoided.

25. All final exports must pass internal review before release.

26. CODEV2E reserves the right to reject work that does not meet studio quality standards.

27. Every contributor is responsible for protecting the creative identity of the studio.

28. The studio prioritizes meaningful storytelling over trend chasing.

29. Team collaboration is mandatory during production and revision stages.

30. Failure to follow studio standards may result in removal from the project or production team.
